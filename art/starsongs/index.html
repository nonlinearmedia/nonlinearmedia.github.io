Minutely proton incidence data, courtesy of noaa.gov

The sounds you're hearing are derived from near-real-time data from the US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's free data service.

Every minute, Earth is bombarded by subatomic particles thrown at us from all over the cosmos, especially from the sun. Our telescopes measure the intensity of this bombardment and beam that information back to earth. Public service sites like noaa aggregate this data and make them available to everyone.

Column 8 in the data below gives proton intensity by the minute:

https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/ace-swepam.txt

I track that column of minute-by-minute data and, starting from the most recent measurement (this minute) and working backwards, emit one word for each measurement by comparing it to the measurement considered immediately prior: LEAST, LESS, SAME, MORE, OR MOST (plus LOW and HIGH for threshold breaches)

In the front-end js, I let user of the app enter a bunch of words like "less", "more", etc., matching the user's lines (from the front-end) with the list of words in the previous step (from-the-back-end at noaa, post-processed in the js) to generate a series of numbers. Then I convert the lines in the previous (data) step into numbers corresponding to the user's input words from the front-end. Finally, I use these numbers as proxies for notes to play from a chosen scale.

Hope you enjoy it. Feel free to fork. There's no hidden code in this app.
