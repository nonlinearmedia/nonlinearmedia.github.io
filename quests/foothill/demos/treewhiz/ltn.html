LTN - Loceff Tree Notation

The Loceff Tree Notation is a notation for specifying General Trees. It is due to CS Professor Michael Loceff, who used this notation extensively in his teaching of CS1B, CS2B, CS1C and CS2C. It's an alternative to JSON for our specifc purpose, tends to be less verbose and more easily typed into or edited in the Tree Visualization tool.

LTN in a Nutshell:

  1. Nodes are named by simply typing them out one per line
  2. Child nodes are specified by hanging them off parent nodes - indenting them in by exactly one space.
  3. The root node name starts at column 0 (first column)
